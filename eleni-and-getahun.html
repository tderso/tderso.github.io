About Eleni Meshesha and Getahun Kebede

I have known Eleni Meshesha Moges and Getahun Kersa Kebede and their respective families for a very long time.

About the author

The author of this narrative is an old man now aged 70 (will turn 71 next May). He is a late deafened person having lost his hearing a month before he turned 19 years old. By then, he had completed his 12th grade education and lost his hearing a week before he was scheduled to seat for the Ethiopian Schools Leaving Certificate Examination (ESLCE). The examination was used to select students who could join the university.

After he lost his hearing, he was taken to a school for the deaf where he was taught Sign Language. He was then assigned as a teacher of the deaf, a task which he left after two months because of a very bad situation that he observed on how the school was treating deaf students.

He sat for the ESLCE, which he had missed the previous year and he was able to join the Haileselassie First University and has graduated with Bachelor of Science degree in Statistics with Mathematics as a minor area of study. He has worked in a governmental organization (for two years), the ENAD (for for three years) and an international organization (for nearly 27 years) until his separation through retirement in 2010.

During his time as a student, along with other deaf persons, he founded the Ethiopian National Association of the Deaf (ENAD). Ever since that time, he has lived and interacted with deaf persons.

Because of all the above, there are many deaf who take him as role model and he need to be careful how he acts and strives very hard to act with honesty to the best of his ability.

Getahun Kersa Kebede

It was when I stayed in the school for the deaf that I came to know Getahun. At one time, I was even Getahun's teacher for a very brief period when he was a fourth grade student. My contacts with Getahun continued even after I left the school after we founded the ENAD. Our contacts has continued even after he moved to the USA.

This has given me to know Getahun very closely. I know him to be a very honest and hard working individual. Before he moved to the USA following his winning of the US Diversity Visa Lottery, Getahun used to own a book binding business in Addis Ababa. He was able to create employment opportunities to other deaf persons. Even after he left for the USA, we have kept contacts first by post, then by e-mail and now through instant messages.

Having been an elder at his first marriage, I was sad to learn about the divorce from his former wife. But, I knew it could not be helped as his former wife refused to listen to all mediation efforts.

After his divorce, I was one of his friends who were urging him to consider marrying again. So, I was pleasantly surprised when he asked for my opinion on a woman he was considering to marry.

Eleni Meshesha Moges

Before I came to know Eleni, I used to know her eldest sister and her other siblings. I also knew her father, Ato Meshesha Moges. When I finally met Eleni, she was a toddler. The toddler I first met grew up to be a very good enterpreneur. Many women (deaf and none deaf) were given opportunities to operate small scale businesses through support provided by the International Labor Organization (ILO). Many failed. But, Eleni, inspite of her deafness, came out a winner of an award by ILO. Her beads making business has prospered and she has been training other women in beads making by using local materials that would be environmental hazards if discarded and littered through an ILO project. Eleni was able to finance her first condominium apartment house through her business earnings, while also supporting her former jobless husband (RIP) and their two children.

Beads on display

Eleni presented her beads for sale at the bazaar that was organized by ILO from 11 February to 6 March 2006.

Award Ceremony

Eleni was one of the women in the ILO project who was selected for the award following her excellent performance in implementing her project.

Interview

Eleni had a media interview after the award ceremony.

The parents of her former husband, Ato Alemayehu Mamo Robi, had some land in Addis Ababa. As one of the heirs, he was entitled to some of the proceeds from the sale of the land. His family knew that if they give him the money, he will drink it away and waste it. So, they made sure to give the money to Eleni, which she used to buy another condominium apartment. This secomd condo has now become a source of income from rent.

It also happened that the parents of Eleni's former husband were farmers. Their farming lands were taken over by the expansion of Addis Ababa. The Addis Ababa administration came up with a policy of conpensating the children of the former farmers whose lands were eaten over by the city. As a result, Eleni's two children and Eleni as their guardian, were able to be granted title deed to a 150 square meters plot of land in Addis Ababa, which has now the estimated value of three million Birr.

It was a very shocking incident to every one when Ato Alemayehu died on 16 April 2018. but, giving his habitual drinking, probably it was not unexpected. The result, Eleni was widowed at such a young age with two under age children to take care of alone.

Getahun asked for my opinion about Eleni

Getahun and Eleni are not strangers. They have known each other for a lomg time when they were married to their respective spouses. I cannot say when they started to get interested on each other. But, I guess the Getahun and Eleni must have started earlier from before the date Getahun asked me about Eleni. I also think that Getahun started to get interested after learning about the death of Eleni's husband. What ever the circumstances might have been, I got a text message from Getahun on 23 August 2018 telling me he would like to marry Eleni and what I think about her attitude. There are only four months between the date Eleni's former husband died and the date Getahun asked me about Eleni. So, I shared my views about Eleni with Getahun. From the above exchange with Getahun, it is obvious to me that Getahun wanted to marry Eleni and that Eleni was not entertaining any idea about going to the USA prior to that. After they got married, Getahun wanted her to join him in the USA.

Civil marriage is registered at Vital Registration Office

The seriousness of Eleni and Getahun about their marriage could be attested by the fact that they wanted us to perform for them all things that our culture and tradition requires under the circumstance. The most enduring marriages are arranged marriages. Althouth we were aware that Eleni and Getahun were contacting each other on a daily basis and their love for each other was growing, they wanted to demonstrate to their respective families that they were highly commited to each other. That can only be done by following all the traditional steps our society considers to bind a man and a woman as husband and wife.

One way to make a marriage binding in the eyes of society and the law is to get the marriage registered. In fact, according to the Family Law of Ethiopia, a marriage should be registered, failing that, if a man and a woman live together for three years, in the eyes of the law, they are considered to be married. So, we had gone to to the "Woreda" (locality) where Eleni resides and asked the Vital Registration officials what Eleni and Getahun need to do to register a civil marriage. We were told that Eleni must submit to them photos of herself and Getahun. They told us that the photos will be posted at their notice board and invite any member of the public to lodge their complaints if there is any one who opposes the marriage from being registered.

 

Vital Registration Office asked for photos of Eleni and Getahun to be posted at their notice board inviting any member of the public who opposes the registration of the marriage to come forwards.

 

Photos given to Vital Registration Office. Secured an appointment for the registration ceremony to take place on 7 Nocember 2018.

 

We discussed about the need to send elders to Eleni's family and ask for her hand. The elders undertook the task on 28 October 2018.

Elders went to ask for Eleni's hand

Once Getahun and Eleni agreed to marry each other, Getahun told me about his wish to send elders to Eleni's family to ask for her hand. His uncle Ato Mekonnen Ajebe, his brother Ato Dawit Kebede and myself met and discussed about the matter. Then, we agreed on a date and we communicated with Eleni to advise her family that elders were going to come. I was included because I am well acquainted with both families.

As the custom dictates, we carried some token presents (A bottle of Whiskey and one kilogram of high quality Tigrai Honey known as "Welela"). After making all the necessary arrangements, the elders paid a visit to Eleni's family on 28 October 2018.

We stated the object of our visit to the family. Since nearly all the siblings of Eleni know Getahun very well, we did not encounter any problems. Our proposal for the two families to unit through marriage was accepted and the elders were entertained by the family.

A date for the wedding was agreed. It was also agreed that the marriage will be registered at the Vital Registration Office at which two elders from each side of the family will be required to sign on the register. It was further agreed that Getahun and Eleni will have to take HIV tests and produce certificates which will be required to be submitted to the Vital Registration Office.
